# Thread: Portfolio Manager Compensation

**Hook:** Your mutual fund manager might get paid the same whether they beat the market or trail it by 5%. The SEC requires them to disclose this. Nobody checks.

---

**1/**
Every mutual fund is required to file a Statement of Additional Information with the SEC. It's the companion document to the prospectus. The difference: the prospectus gets mailed to you. The SAI is available "upon request."

Most investors don't know it exists.

**2/**
Buried in the SAI is a section on portfolio manager compensation. The SEC requires funds to describe the structure and method of computing PM compensation, including whether it's tied to fund performance, assets under management, or overall firm revenue.

**3/**
Some fund managers are paid based on performance relative to a benchmark. Their incentives are aligned with yours: beat the index, earn more.

Others are paid based on AUM. The fund gets bigger, they earn more. Performance is irrelevant to their paycheck. Their incentive is asset gathering, not returns.

**4/**
This creates a structural misalignment. A manager compensated on AUM benefits from marketing and platform distribution — getting the fund onto recommended lists and 401k menus. They do not benefit from outperformance. They benefit from being large.

A manager compensated on performance benefits from being good.

**5/**
The data to distinguish between these two types of managers exists. It's in the SAI. It's filed with the SEC. It's publicly available.

But nobody has built a tool to extract and compare PM compensation structures across funds, until now.

**6/**
Fund Autopsy is building an SAI parser to surface exactly this. Search a fund, see how the person managing your money gets paid. Compare it across your entire portfolio.

The data has been there all along. We're digging it up.
